The European Parliament has approved the 'Chat Control 1.0' regulation, allowing US tech companies to perform mass scanning of private messages without warrants until 2028. This decision was made despite a majority of voting MEPs (314 against, 276 in favor) opposing it, because the motion to reject it failed to secure the required absolute majority of 361 votes. This legislation represents a significant erosion of digital privacy in the EU, permitting surveillance of private communications on major platforms like Instagram, Discord, and Gmail. It sets a precedent for bypassing majority democratic will through procedural rules and raises concerns about the normalization of mass surveillance under the guise of child protection. The regulation was reactivated under a procedural 'default acceptance' rule on the last session day before the summer break, requiring an absolute majority of all MEPs (not just those voting) to reject it; 113 MEPs were absent. It permits scanning via 'client-side scanning' technology, which analyzes message content on the user's device before sending.

The current 'version 1.0' is an extension of temporary derogations first introduced in 2021.

## [Colibrì Project Runs GLM 5.2 LLM on Standard 32GB RAM Computer](https://github.com/JustVugg/colibri) ⭐️ 7.0/10

A developer successfully ran Z.AI's 744-billion parameter GLM 5.2 Mixture-of-Experts model on a personal laptop with only 32GB of RAM by creating an inference engine that uses int4 quantization and streams model experts from disk, achieving a functional but slow 0.1 tokens/second. This project demonstrates the feasibility of running extremely large frontier models on consumer-grade hardware without a GPU, potentially democratizing access to powerful LLMs for experimentation and development, even with significant performance trade-offs. The Colibrì engine is a single C file that keeps the model's dense part (~17B parameters) resident in RAM at int4 quantization (~9.9 GB) and streams the 21,504 routed experts from disk using an LRU cache, but it warns that this can cause significant SSD wear.

hackernews · vforno · Jul 9, 08:05 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48842459)

**Background**: GLM 5.2 is a flagship long-horizon task model from Z.AI with a massive 744B parameter Mixture-of-Experts architecture, meaning only a fraction of its parameters are active per token. Techniques like Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) and Dynamic Sparse Attention (DSA) are used to optimize long-context inference. Quantization, such as converting model weights from 16-bit to 4-bit integers (int4), is a common method to reduce the memory footprint of large models.
